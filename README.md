# India CPI Inflation & Global Oil Price Analysis (2017 - 2023)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project is an end-to-end data analytics case study exploring how retail inflation moves in India and tracking whether global crude oil prices actually drive up daily living costs for the common man. Using **Advanced Excel** and **Pivot Tables**, I cleaned raw economic data, calculated month-on-month changes, isolated seasonal spikes, and ran statistical correlation tests to see the bigger picture.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Skills Used
* **Data Cleaning:** Excel Power Query & formatting raw dates.
* **Analysis:** Pivot Tables, AVERAGEIFS formulas, and =CORREL() matrix functions.
* **Data Visualization:** Built dynamic line charts, pie charts, and horizontal bar charts to map trends.

---

## 📊 Key Findings & Visuals

### 1. Where does the money go? (Category Breakdown)
Nearly half (**48.31%**) of the consumer index weight goes straight to **Food and Beverages**, with the **Miscellaneous** bucket taking up **23.79%**. Together, these make up over 72% of the entire index. This proves that the common budget in India is heavily tied to basic daily living costs.

### 2. The Long-Term Trend (Year-on-Year)
Inflation was crawling up slowly from 2017 (2.74%) to 2019 (3.70%). Then, the pandemic hit, causing a massive, high plateau over 2020 (6.56%) and **2021 (the true peak at 6.60%)** due to heavy lockdown supply chain breaks. Prices finally started cooling down slightly in 2022 (6.25%).

### 3. Food Price Rollercoaster
Over the 12 months ending in May 2023, food inflation hit its highest monthly jump in **June 2022 (1.04%)** and its lowest drop in **February 2023 (-0.66%)**. Looking closer, **Spices** was the absolute biggest driver of price hikes (+30.90 points), while **Oils and fats** dropped the most (-30.90 points). This shows how heavily vulnerable food costs are to seasonal weather and monsoons.

### 4. The COVID-19 Shock
Looking right at the March 2020 lockdown baseline, categories completely split up: **Food** spiked immediately due to panic buying and broken truck routes, **Health** costs launched a steady, permanent march upward from medical resource strain, and **Fuel & Light** crashed immediately because of the industrial standstill before recovering late in the year.

### 5. The Global Oil Connection
The correlation math showed that while **Fuel & Light** was the most sensitive to global crude oil shifts, its score was still incredibly low at **0.123**. Even crazier, **Food (0.06)** and **Miscellaneous (0.03)** showed almost zero mathematical connection. This proves that domestic price shields successfully blocked global oil shocks from directly hitting everyday consumer items.

## 🚀 Practical Recommendations

* **Make Everyday Food Cheaper to Move:** Build direct digital connections between farmers and local markets to cut out unnecessary middle-men who inflate retail prices along the way.
* **Prepare Backup Shipping Routes for Big Crises:** Set up alternative trucking and shipping networks ahead of time so that if a main highway or transport corridor gets blocked, backup routes take over instantly.
* **Build Proper Storage to Keep Food Fresh:** Build a large network of solar-powered cold storage warehouses right next to major farming hubs to store excess supply during great harvests, keeping prices steady year-round.
* **Keep National Emergency Stocks:** Maintain a permanent, rolling national reserve of long-lasting food staples and critical medical gear to stop panic-buying and price gouging during crises.
* **Switch to Targeted Fuel Discounts:** Move away from blanket fuel discounts and give subsidies *only* to commercial delivery trucks, public buses, and lower-income families to save the country's budget while protecting transport routes.
